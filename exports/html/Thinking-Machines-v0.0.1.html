Investment Memo: Thinking Machines Lab

Company: Thinking Machines Lab

Stage: Seed (Seeking Follow-On)
Location: San Francisco, California
Website: https://thinkingmachines.ai/
Date: November 2024

1. Executive Summary

Thinking Machines Lab, founded in early 2025 by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati (LinkedIn) and researcher Lilian Weng (LinkedIn), is seeking follow-on funding at a $50-60B valuation—4-5x its July 2025 seed valuation of $10-12B12. The company raised the largest seed round in Crunchbase history ($2B from Andreessen Horowitz (website), NVIDIA (website), and others)345 and recently launched its first product, Tinker, an API for automating custom frontier AI model creation67.

The investment case rests almost entirely on team pedigree and market momentum rather than demonstrated traction8. With less than one year of operation, no disclosed revenue or customer metrics, and one co-founder already departed (Andrew Tulloch (LinkedIn) left for Meta (website) in October 2025), the company represents an extreme valuation relative to execution91011. The $50-60B target would value Thinking Machines higher than most publicly-traded enterprise software companies despite having a single API product in market.

2. Business Overview

What they do: Thinking Machines Lab builds multimodal AI systems and tools that enable developers and researchers to customize and fine-tune frontier AI models through accessible APIs121314.

Problem they solve: Fine-tuning cutting-edge AI models currently requires specialized expertise, significant compute resources, and deep understanding of model architectures1516. This creates a barrier between frontier AI capabilities (available only at labs like OpenAI (website), Anthropic (website), Google (website)) and the broader developer community seeking to customize these models for specific use cases.

Solution approach: The company’s first product, Tinker, is an API that automates the creation of custom frontier AI models1718. The tool aims to “demystify the work involved in tuning powerful AI models” and make frontier capabilities accessible without requiring deep ML expertise19. Their broader vision centers on human-AI collaboration rather than fully autonomous systems2021.

Business model: Likely API-based pricing (similar to OpenAI, Anthropic models), though specific pricing, unit economics, and revenue model remain undisclosed22. The company has secured an OpenAI services partnership focused on ChatGPT enterprise adoption in APAC, suggesting potential services revenue alongside API fees23.

3. Market Context

Total Addressable Market (TAM): Specific market sizing unavailable, but the company operates at the intersection of several large markets: AI infrastructure tooling, model customization/fine-tuning services, and enterprise AI adoption. The broader AI software market was estimated at $150B in 2023 (McKinsey), growing at 35-40% CAGR24.

Market dynamics: - Capital abundance for AI labs: Investors continue deploying massive capital into next-generation AI companies, with early-stage startups achieving unicorn ($1B+) valuations within months of founding—a trend Thinking Machines exemplifies at the extreme end2526. - Fine-tuning as emerging category: As foundation models commoditize, customization and fine-tuning represent the next value layer. Companies increasingly need domain-specific models rather than general-purpose LLMs2728. - Enterprise AI acceleration in APAC: Growing demand for AI implementation support in Asia-Pacific markets, where Thinking Machines has positioned as OpenAI’s first regional services partner29. - Shift toward human-AI collaboration: Market moving away from “AI will replace humans” narrative toward augmentation and collaboration tools3031.

Competitive landscape:

Direct competitors: - Hugging Face (website) (valued at $4.5B in 2023): Offers model hosting, fine-tuning APIs, and developer tools with 1M+ users323334 - Weights & Biases (website) ($1B+ valuation): MLOps platform including model fine-tuning capabilities - Replicate (website) ($125M raised): API platform for running and fine-tuning open-source models - Together AI (website) ($100M+ raised): Decentralized cloud for training and fine-tuning models

Indirect alternatives: - Native fine-tuning APIs from OpenAI (website), Anthropic (website), Google Vertex AI (website) - Open-source frameworks (Hugging Face Transformers, PyTorch Lightning) - Enterprise ML platforms (Databricks (website), AWS SageMaker (website))

Differentiation: Team pedigree from OpenAI provides credibility and potential access to frontier research353637. Focus on “multimodal” and “human-AI collaboration” differentiates from pure infrastructure plays, though specifics remain vague3839. OpenAI partnership for APAC enterprise adoption suggests go-to-market advantage in that region40.

4. Technology & Product

Technical approach: Multimodal AI systems designed for human-AI collaboration, with emphasis on making frontier model capabilities accessible through simplified interfaces414243. The company focuses on fine-tuning existing frontier models rather than training foundation models from scratch—a capital-efficient approach that leverages work from OpenAI, Anthropic, and others4445.

Current product: Tinker (launched 2025) is an API that automates custom frontier AI model creation4647. The tool abstracts away complexity of model fine-tuning, allowing developers to create specialized versions of powerful base models without deep ML expertise4849. Specific technical details (supported base models, fine-tuning techniques, performance benchmarks) are not publicly disclosed50.

Product roadmap: - Expand accessibility of frontier AI capabilities to broader researcher and developer audiences5152 - Build additional multimodal systems emphasizing collaborative workflows5354 - “Push technical boundaries while delivering real value to as many people as possible” (company mission statement—notably vague on specifics)55

Technical risks/challenges: - Dependency on third-party models: If fine-tuning frontier models from OpenAI/Anthropic/Google, the company’s value proposition relies on continued API access and favorable pricing from potential competitors5657. - Differentiation sustainability: Fine-tuning APIs are increasingly commoditized—OpenAI, Anthropic, and Hugging Face all offer similar capabilities. Unclear what technical moat Thinking Machines can build beyond execution speed5859. - Multimodal complexity: Building robust multimodal systems (text, image, video, audio) requires significantly more engineering than text-only models. No evidence yet of multimodal capabilities in Tinker60.

8. Risks & Mitigations

(All risks substantiated by fundraise metrics, team disclosures, or public investor statements131132133134)

  1. Valuation Risk: $50-60B valuation with no disclosed revenue, customers, or product-market fit represents extreme premium on team pedigree alone135136.
  2. Team Stability Risk: Co-founder Andrew Tulloch (LinkedIn) departed for Meta (website) in October 2025, just months after founding and immediately post-$2B raise137138.
  3. Product Differentiation Risk: Fine-tuning APIs are increasingly commoditized. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Hugging Face all offer similar capabilities139140.
  4. Traction Transparency Risk: Zero disclosed metrics (revenue, customers, API usage, retention)141142.
  5. Market Timing Risk: The company is riding peak AI hype cycle. Vertical integration risk from foundation model providers143144.
  6. Dependency Risk: If Tinker relies on third-party foundation models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta), company faces pricing power and access risks145146.
  7. Execution Risk: Neither founder has scaled a commercial company147148149.
  8. Capital Efficiency Risk: $2B seed round suggests high burn rate; no disclosed revenue150151.

9. Investment Thesis

Why now: The AI infrastructure market is experiencing unprecedented capital inflow, with investors aggressively backing experienced teams from leading labs152153. Foundation models are commoditizing, creating opportunity for differentiation in the fine-tuning and customization layer. Enterprise AI adoption is accelerating, particularly in APAC where Thinking Machines has positioning through the OpenAI partnership154.

Why this team: Mira Murati’s (LinkedIn) six-year tenure as OpenAI CTO during ChatGPT’s development and scaling provides rare operational experience with frontier AI systems155156157158. The founding team’s OpenAI pedigree offers credibility with customers, recruiting advantages, and potential access to cutting-edge research159160161. Lilian Weng’s (LinkedIn) technical depth in model training and safety adds research credibility162163.

10. Recommendation

Decision: PASS (Strong reconsider at Series A with traction)
