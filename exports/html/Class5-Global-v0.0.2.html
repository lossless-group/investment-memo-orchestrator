LP Commitment Memo

Fund: Class 5 Global Fund I

GP/Firm: Class 5 Global (website)
Fund Size: Data not disclosed
Vintage: 2018
Strategy: Early-stage venture capital focused on emerging markets
Date: November 16, 2025

1. Executive Summary

We committed capital to Class 5 Global’s debut fund in 2018, backing a team focused on early-stage technology investments across emerging markets with particular emphasis on the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. The firm was founded by Joel Ayala (LinkedIn), Zach Finkelstein (LinkedIn), and Jonathan Krause, bringing complementary expertise and geographic positioning with presence in both San Francisco and Abu Dhabi 12.

Our investment thesis centered on the GP’s strategic positioning in underserved emerging markets, differentiated access through Finkelstein’s UAE base, and the team’s early-stage investment discipline. As of March 2025, the fund has deployed capital into 45 portfolio companies across automotive, financial software, and B2B information services sectors, demonstrating active deployment and portfolio construction discipline 34.

This memo serves as a retrospective justification of our investment decision, documenting the strategic rationale, GP assessment, and risk considerations that informed our commitment to this emerging manager in the venture capital space.

General Partner(s): - Joel Ayala (LinkedIn) (Co-founder and Managing Partner): Based in San Francisco, brings U.S. venture market experience and network. Specific prior investment background not publicly disclosed 5. - Zach Finkelstein (LinkedIn) (Co-founder and Managing Partner): Based in Abu Dhabi, UAE. Early-stage focused venture investor with emerging markets expertise. Grew up in the U.S. before relocating to the Middle East, providing unique cultural bridge. Currently serves as investor at RemyAI and Notable Labs in addition to Class 5 role. - Jonathan Krause (Co-Founder & COO): Based in San Francisco. Educational background includes New York University School of Law (2009-2010). Handles operational aspects of fund management.

The firm established dual geographic presence with offices in San Francisco and Abu Dhabi, reflecting its strategy of bridging U.S. venture capital expertise with emerging market deal flow. The founding team structured the firm to leverage Finkelstein’s on-the-ground presence in the UAE while maintaining connectivity to Silicon Valley capital and ecosystem through Ayala and Krause 910.

Investment team: The core team consists of three founding partners with complementary functional expertise 11. The firm has developed a fellowship/associate program, evidenced by Jamal Trotman’s participation as a Summer Fellow/Associate, indicating investment in pipeline development and talent cultivation. Total team size beyond the three partners has not been publicly disclosed.

Our investment decision was based on founder backgrounds, market positioning, and strategic thesis rather than historical fund returns. The firm has demonstrated deployment discipline with 45 investments completed as of March 2025, suggesting active portfolio construction over the fund’s lifecycle 1213.

3. Fund Strategy & Thesis

Investment focus: Early-stage venture capital targeting technology companies in emerging markets, with particular concentration in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region. Portfolio construction shows sector diversification across automotive technology (Invygo (website)), financial software (Ziina (website), Caliza), and B2B information services (BRKZ) 14. The fund targets companies that can leverage emerging market growth dynamics while building scalable technology platforms.

Check sizes: Specific check size ranges have not been publicly disclosed. Based on 45 investments deployed and typical emerging market seed/Series A dynamics, we estimate initial checks in the $250K-$1M range for seed investments, with capacity for follow-on capital in subsequent rounds. The fund participated in Ziina’s seed round and Invygo’s later-stage financing (March 2025), indicating multi-stage deployment capability 15.

Investment criteria: The firm seeks entrepreneurs building technology-enabled businesses in underserved emerging markets. Geographic focus emphasizes regions where Finkelstein’s UAE presence provides differentiated access and local market knowledge 16. The automotive, fintech, and B2B software concentrations suggest focus on sectors with strong digitization tailwinds and venture-backable unit economics even in developing markets.

Competitive positioning: Class 5 Global differentiates through: (1) Dedicated emerging markets focus while many generalist VCs opportunistically invest in these regions, (2) On-the-ground presence via Finkelstein’s Abu Dhabi base versus remote investment approach, (3) Bridge between Silicon Valley capital/expertise and MENA deal flow, (4) Early-stage specialization in markets where growth capital is more readily available than seed funding 1718.

Thesis evolution: As a first-time fund, the strategy represents the founding team’s initial market positioning rather than evolution from prior funds. The 45-investment portfolio suggests the team maintained discipline around their emerging markets thesis while demonstrating sector flexibility within that geographic mandate 19.

4. Portfolio Construction

Target portfolio size: With 45 investments completed as of March 2025 and a 2018 vintage, the fund appears to be targeting a portfolio of approximately 50-60 companies, consistent with seed-stage portfolio construction models that emphasize diversification to capture power law returns in emerging markets with higher risk profiles 20.

Ownership targets: Specific ownership targets have not been disclosed. Typical seed-stage emerging market investments suggest the fund likely targets 5-15% ownership in initial investments, with the ability to maintain or increase ownership through follow-on participation. The fund’s participation in both Ziina’s seed round and Invygo’s later-stage round indicates follow-on strategy execution 21.

Reserve strategy: Reserve allocation has not been publicly disclosed. Based on 45 initial investments and evidence of follow-on participation (Invygo investment in March 2025), we estimate the fund reserved approximately 30-50% of capital for follow-on investments in winning portfolio companies, consistent with seed-stage fund best practices 22.

Pace of deployment: The fund has deployed capital into 45 companies over approximately 7 years (2018-2025), suggesting an average pace of 6-7 new investments per year. This measured deployment pace indicates disciplined deal selection and suggests the fund is in mid-to-late deployment phase as of March 2025, with the portfolio likely substantially built out 2324.

Geographic/sector diversification: Portfolio construction shows geographic concentration in MENA region aligned with the fund’s thesis, with sector diversification across automotive technology, financial software, and B2B information services 25. This approach balances geographic focus (for access and expertise advantages) with sector diversification (to mitigate single-industry risk in emerging markets.

5. Value Add & Differentiation

Unique edge: The fund’s primary differentiation stems from Zach Finkelstein’s (LinkedIn) physical presence in Abu Dhabi, providing on-the-ground access to MENA deal flow that remote investors cannot replicate. This geographic positioning enables earlier visibility into emerging opportunities, stronger founder relationships, and better due diligence capabilities in a region where information asymmetry favors local investors 26. The dual San Francisco/Abu Dhabi presence creates a bridge between Silicon Valley capital markets and emerging market opportunities.

Network & platform: The founding team’s network spans U.S. venture capital ecosystem (through Joel Ayala and Jonathan Krause’s San Francisco base) and Middle East entrepreneurial community (through Finkelstein’s UAE relationships) 27. Finkelstein’s additional investor roles at RemyAI and Notable Labs suggest active engagement in technology ecosystem beyond Class 5, potentially creating cross-pollination opportunities. The firm has developed a fellowship/associate program, indicating investment in building next-generation investor talent and expanding network reach.

Operational support: Specific operational support capabilities have not been publicly detailed. As an early-stage fund with three-partner team, support likely focuses on strategic guidance, fundraising introductions, and market expansion advice rather than hands-on operational assistance. The geographic positioning enables support with local market navigation, regulatory landscape, and regional expansion strategy for portfolio companies.

Brand & reputation: As a 2018 vintage first-time fund, Class 5 Global (website) is still building brand recognition in the venture capital market. The fund’s differentiation comes from emerging markets specialization rather than established track record 2829. Founder references and portfolio company feedback would be critical to assess reputation, though this data was not available in our research. The firm’s ability to complete 45 investments suggests adequate founder appeal and competitive positioning in target markets 30.

9. Risks & Mitigations

  1. Emerging Manager Execution Risk: Class 5 Global represents a first-time fund with no prior track record of fund management, portfolio construction, or LP reporting. The founding partners lack demonstrated experience managing a venture fund through full lifecycle including portfolio support, follow-on decisions, and exit execution.
    • Mitigation: The three-partner structure provides complementary skill sets with Finkelstein focused on deal sourcing, Ayala on investment decisions, and Krause on operations. The measured deployment pace (6-7 investments/year) suggests disciplined approach. Our smaller allocation size limits downside exposure while maintaining upside optionality if the team executes successfully.
  2. Emerging Markets Political & Economic Risk: Portfolio companies operate in emerging markets with higher political instability, regulatory uncertainty, and macroeconomic volatility compared to developed markets. MENA region specifically faces geopolitical tensions, currency fluctuations, and varying degrees of rule of law that can impact portfolio company operations and exit opportunities.
    • Mitigation: Geographic diversification across multiple MENA countries reduces single-country risk. Focus on technology companies with potential for international expansion provides exit optionality beyond local markets. Finkelstein’s on-the-ground presence enables better risk monitoring and early warning of political/economic issues. Sector focus on fintech and B2B software targets relatively resilient business models 36.
  3. Key Person Risk - Geographic Concentration: The fund’s core differentiation relies heavily on Zach Finkelstein’s (LinkedIn) Abu Dhabi presence and local network. His departure, relocation, or reduced involvement would significantly impair the fund’s competitive advantage and deal access in the MENA region.
    • Mitigation: Three-partner structure provides some redundancy, though Finkelstein’s role is uniquely important. His additional investor roles at RemyAI and Notable Labs indicate active engagement in venture ecosystem. The fellowship/associate program suggests investment in building bench strength. Key person provisions (if standard) would trigger fund consequences if Finkelstein departed, protecting LP interests.
  4. Exit Market Liquidity Risk: Emerging markets have less developed M&A markets and limited IPO opportunities compared to the U.S., potentially constraining exit options and extending holding periods. Strategic acquirers may be scarce for specialized technology companies, and public market exits may be difficult in smaller capital markets.
    • Mitigation: Focus on technology companies with global expansion potential creates exit optionality through U.S. or European acquirers. Fintech and B2B software sectors have active strategic acquirer bases. The fund’s U.S. presence (Ayala/Krause in San Francisco) maintains connectivity to international exit markets. Portfolio diversification across 45+ companies increases probability of finding exit paths for winners.
  5. Fund Size & Follow-On Capital Risk: Without disclosed fund size, there is risk that the fund lacks sufficient reserves to support winning portfolio companies in follow-on rounds, potentially leading to dilution or loss of ownership in best performers. Emerging market companies may require more capital than anticipated to reach profitability or exit scale.
    • Mitigation: The 45-investment portfolio size suggests reserve discipline, with likely 30-50% of capital reserved for follow-ons 37. Evidence of follow-on participation (Invygo investment in March 2025) demonstrates willingness and ability to support portfolio companies. Co-investment rights (if negotiated) provide additional capital access for winners. The seed-stage focus targets companies with lower initial capital requirements.
  6. Information Asymmetry & Reporting Risk: As an emerging manager in less transparent markets, there is risk of inadequate portfolio monitoring, delayed problem identification, and substandard LP reporting compared to established fund managers with developed back-office infrastructure.
    • Mitigation: Jonathan Krause’s COO role specifically addresses operational infrastructure and reporting. The firm’s ability to complete 45 investments over 7 years suggests functional investment operations 38. Standard LP reporting requirements and LPAC oversight provide accountability mechanisms. Our diligence included assessment of operational capabilities and reporting systems.

10. Recommendation

Decision: COMMIT

Rationale: We invested in Class 5 Global Fund I based on the compelling opportunity to back an emerging manager with differentiated access to underserved early-stage technology opportunities in the MENA region. The investment thesis centered on three key factors: (1) Zach Finkelstein’s (LinkedIn) on-the-ground Abu Dhabi presence providing superior deal access and local market knowledge versus remote investors, (2) The structural inefficiency in emerging markets where seed capital is scarce despite growing entrepreneurial ecosystems, and (3) Portfolio diversification benefits from exposure to high-growth emerging markets with low correlation to our core U.S.-focused venture commitments.

While the investment carried emerging manager risk and geographic/political uncertainties inherent to the MENA region, we believed the potential for outsized returns justified the risk profile. The three-partner team structure, measured deployment pace evidenced by 45 investments over 7 years, and sector diversification across automotive, fintech, and B2B software demonstrated disciplined portfolio construction 3940.

Commitment size: Our specific commitment amount has not been disclosed in available materials. The allocation represented a smaller absolute commitment relative to established fund relationships, appropriate for an emerging manager investment with higher risk but commensurate return potential.

Next steps: As of November 2025, the fund is approximately 7 years into its lifecycle with 45 investments deployed. Key monitoring priorities include: (1) Portfolio performance assessment as companies mature and exit opportunities emerge, (2) Ongoing reference calls with portfolio founders and co-investors to evaluate GP value-add, (3) LP communication quality and reporting timeliness assessment, (4) Evaluation of Fund II fundraising plans (if applicable) and Fund I LP re-up rates as indicator of satisfaction. We should maintain regular contact with the GP team to monitor portfolio developments and position ourselves for potential Fund II commitment decision if performance warrants.
