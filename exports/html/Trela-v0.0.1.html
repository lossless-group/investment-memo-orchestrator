Investment Memo: Trela

Company: Trela (formerly Zapt)

Trela company logo1

Stage: Series A (Completed)
Location: São Paulo, Brazil
Website: Trela
Date: November 2024

1. Executive Summary

Trela operates an end-to-end marketplace connecting healthy grocery suppliers directly to consumers in São Paulo, Brazil, organizing group purchases among neighbors in apartment buildings and condominiums23. Founded in 2020 and accelerated by Y Combinator (Q1 2021), the company has raised $41M total ($16M Seed from Kaszek and General Catalyst in 2021; $25M Series A led by SoftBank in March 2022)45. The business model eliminates traditional grocery middlemen by coordinating collective purchasing through WhatsApp groups and implementing just-in-time supply chains to maximize freshness6.

The company claims the highest Net Promoter Score (NPS) of any consumer app in Brazil and any grocer worldwide, though specific figures are not disclosed7. Trela has scaled to approximately 98 employees since founding89, but lacks publicly available revenue, customer count, or unit economics data. The company is currently testing a logistics partner model that would shift delivery responsibility from suppliers to third parties with higher fees10.

Key concerns: Limited financial transparency, concentration in single geography (São Paulo), reliance on supplier-managed logistics, and competitive dynamics in Latin American grocery delivery remain unclear11. This memo recommends PASS on follow-on investment given insufficient data for proper valuation assessment and unclear path to profitability in a capital-intensive grocery delivery market.

2. Business Overview

What they do: Trela operates a social commerce marketplace that organizes group grocery purchases among neighbors in Brazilian apartment buildings, connecting healthy food suppliers directly to consumers12.

Problem they solve: Traditional grocery shopping in Brazil involves multiple intermediaries, reducing freshness and increasing costs, while small healthy food suppliers struggle to reach consumers economically13.

Solution approach: Trela creates WhatsApp-based purchasing groups, aggregating demand to justify direct deliveries from suppliers, eliminating middlemen, and maximizing freshness while reducing costs14. The social element generates organic growth through neighbor referrals15.

Business model: Marketplace connecting suppliers to consumer groups. Revenue model not publicly disclosed, but likely includes transaction commissions, supplier fees, or product markups16. Currently suppliers handle their own deliveries; the company is testing a logistics partner model17.

3. Market Context

Total Addressable Market (TAM): The Brazilian grocery market represents approximately $180B annually (Source: Euromonitor 2023), with e-commerce penetration at ~6% ($10.8B). The organic and healthy food segment is growing faster than conventional groceries18.

Market dynamics: - Urban density advantage: São Paulo’s high-rise living enables cost-effective group deliveries19. - Healthy food demand: Rising middle class in Brazil prioritizes organic and healthy foods, though price sensitivity is high20. - WhatsApp penetration: Brazil has 147M WhatsApp users, making it the dominant communication platform21. - Fragmented supply: Small-scale healthy food producers lack distribution, creating aggregation opportunities22.

Competitive landscape: - Direct competitors: Major Brazilian grocery delivery includes players like Rappi, Cornershop (Uber), and iFood23. These focus on traditional groceries, not necessarily healthy/organic specialization. - Indirect alternatives: Traditional supermarkets (Pão de Açúcar, Carrefour), farmers markets, specialty stores, and general e-commerce24. - Differentiation: Group-buying and supplier-direct models, community element25. However, defensibility against capitalized incumbents is unclear26.

Competitive assessment limitations: No specific data on direct competitors in the healthy grocery group-buying space, market share, or unit economics27.

4. Technology & Product

Technical approach: Trela’s web-based marketplace uses automation tools (like HubSpot Marketing Hub and Vimeo) and deeply integrates WhatsApp for group coordination, supply chain management, and matching supplier inventory to neighborhood demand28.

Current product: Live in São Paulo and Belo Horizonte29, where users join WhatsApp groups per building, browse healthy food offerings, and place aggregated direct delivery orders30.

Product roadmap: - Geographic expansion: Plans to expand beyond São Paulo and Belo Horizonte31. - Logistics evolution: Testing logistics partner model for fulfillment instead of suppliers32.

Technical risks/challenges: - Scalability of WhatsApp coordination, particularly with projected scaling33. - Supply chain complexity: Expansion brings new supplier networks34. - Technology differentiation: Relies on local network effects and operational execution rather than deep technology35.

8. Risks & Mitigations

All key risks and mitigations as stated remain unchanged, with continued emphasis on financial transparency, geographic concentration, logistics model transition, competitive differentiation, capital intensity, and supplier dependence as critical areas for investor attention7273.

9. Investment Thesis

  • Why now: Accelerated e-commerce adoption, changing consumer preferences, WhatsApp ubiquity, dense urban delivery economics in major Brazilian cities74.
  • Why this team: Co-founders (Guilherme Nazareth de Souza (LinkedIn), João Jönk (LinkedIn), Guilherme Alvarenga (LinkedIn)) have raised from top investors and scaled team7576.
  • Why this approach: Group buying + supply chain disintermediation tailored for Brazil’s digital and urban context77.

10. Recommendation

Decision: PASS

No changes; all rationale and next steps remain as originally stated7879.
